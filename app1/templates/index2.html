Delicious Recipe Tutorial

Recipe Title

Ingredients

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnishing

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Prepare the Sugar Syrup: In a saucepan, add sugar and water. Bring it to a boil and let it cook until you get a one-string consistency. This means that when you touch a drop of the syrup between your thumb and index finger, it should form a single string.

  2. Step 2:Prepare the Gram Flour Mixture: Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan on low-medium heat. Add besan and mix well to form a smooth paste. Keep stirring continuously to roast the besan in the ghee. This step is crucial to prevent the mixture from burning or sticking to the pan. Roast until the besan turns golden brown and you can smell a nutty aroma. This might take around 15-20 minutes.

  3. Step 3: Once the besan is roasted, reduce the heat to low. Gradually add the prepared sugar syrup to the besan mixture while stirring continuously. Be cautious, as the mixture can splatter. Mix well to combine the syrup and besan thoroughly.

  4. Step 4:Add cardamom powder to the mixture and mix well.

  5. Step 5:Shape the Sohan Papdi: Grease a plate or tray with ghee. Pour the mixture onto the greased surface and spread it evenly with the help of a greased spatula. While the mixture is still warm, sprinkle chopped nuts over the surface and gently press them in.

  6. Step 6: Let the mixture cool down for a few minutes but don't let it become completely hard. While it's still warm, cut the mixture into square or diamond shapes using a knife.
